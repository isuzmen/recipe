Cougito

What is Cougito?

Coquito is a traditional holiday drink originating from Puerto Rico. In Spanish, coquito means "nutmeg". The drink is coconut-based and enriched with rum. Rich and creamy coquito, often thickened with egg yolk, is similar to eggnog. In fact, it is sometimes referred to as "Puerto Rican eggnog."

Ingradients

  • Evaporated milk: This coquito recipe starts with a can of evaporated milk.
  • Eggs: Whisked two egg yolks contribute to the rich, thick, creamy texture.
  • Coconut cream : Preserved coconut cream adds richness and gives coquito its distinctive coconut flavor.
  • Sweetened condensed milk: Sweetened condensed milk makes everything taste better, including this coquito recipe!
  • White rum: The best rum for coquito is white Puerto Rican rum, such as Bacardi. In a pinch, any pleasant-tasting white rum will work.
  • Water: Water thins the thick and creamy mixture, making it super pourable.
  • Vanilla: A teaspoon of vanilla extract takes the flavor to the next level.
  • Spices: Cinnamon and cloves add warmth and comfort.

Steps

  1. Cook the evaporated milk and egg yolks in a double boiler until thick.
  2. Transfer the mixture to a blender and blend with the remaining ingredients.
  3. Transfer to glass bottles and refrigerator until cold.
